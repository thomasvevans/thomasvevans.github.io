_A true Roman classic — creamy without cream, rich with egg, pecorino, and guanciale. Carbonara is the soul of simplicity and indulgence._

---

## 📝 Ingredients

- 400g (14 oz) spaghetti  
- 150g (5 oz) guanciale (or pancetta), cut into strips  
- 3 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg  
- 50g (½ cup) grated Pecorino Romano cheese  
- Freshly ground black pepper  
- Salt (for pasta water only)

---

## 👩‍🍳 Instructions

### Step 1: Cook the Pasta  
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente.  
2. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining.

### Step 2: Prepare the Sauce  
1. While pasta cooks, beat the eggs and yolks in a bowl.  
2. Add Pecorino and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Mix until smooth.

### Step 3: Cook the Guanciale  
1. In a large skillet, cook guanciale over medium heat until crispy and golden.  
2. Remove from heat. Add drained pasta directly to the pan with guanciale.

### Step 4: Combine  
1. Off the heat, pour the egg mixture over the hot pasta. Toss quickly and vigorously to coat without scrambling.  
2. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until the sauce becomes silky.

---

## 🍽️ Serving Suggestion

Serve immediately with extra Pecorino and cracked black pepper. Pair with a glass of **Frascati** or **Trebbiano**.

---

## 🧀 Chef's Tip

Never add cream — the creaminess comes from the eggs and cheese. Use **guanciale** if possible for the most authentic Roman flavour.
