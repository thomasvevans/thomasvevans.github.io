_Golden, crispy, and filled with savoury ragu and gooey mozzarella — arancini are the ultimate Sicilian street food snack._

---

## 📝 Ingredients

### For the Rice:
- 1 tbsp olive oil  
- 1 small onion, diced  
- 300g (1½ cups) arborio or carnaroli rice  
- 750ml (3 cups) chicken or vegetable broth  
- Pinch of saffron (optional)  
- 50g (¼ cup) grated Parmigiano  
- Salt to taste

### For the Filling:
- 150g (5 oz) meat ragù (leftover works great)  
- 80g (3 oz) mozzarella, diced  

### For Breading:
- Flour  
- 2 eggs, beaten  
- Breadcrumbs  
- Vegetable oil (for frying)

---

## 👩‍🍳 Instructions

### Step 1: Cook the Rice  
1. In a saucepan, sauté onion in olive oil until soft.  
2. Add rice and toast for 2 minutes.  
3. Slowly add hot broth, stirring, until rice is tender and sticky.  
4. Stir in saffron (if using), Parmigiano, and salt. Let cool completely.

### Step 2: Assemble the Arancini  
1. With damp hands, take a spoonful of rice and flatten it.  
2. Place a bit of ragù and mozzarella in the centre.  
3. Enclose filling with more rice and shape into a ball.

### Step 3: Bread and Fry  
1. Roll each ball in flour, then egg, then breadcrumbs.  
2. Heat oil to 170°C / 340°F. Fry until golden and crispy (about 3–4 mins each).  
3. Drain on paper towels.

---

## 🍷 Serving Suggestion

Serve warm as a snack or appetiser with a glass of **Nero d’Avola** or a chilled **Rosé**.

---

## 🌟 Chef's Tip

Chill the shaped arancini before frying — they’ll hold together better and fry more evenly.
