_Layers of rich ragù, creamy béchamel, and silky pasta sheets — this classic Italian lasagne brings the warmth of a Sunday lunch in Emilia-Romagna straight to your kitchen._

---

### 📝 Ingredients

#### For the Ragù (Meat Sauce):
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small onion, finely diced
- 1 small carrot, finely diced
- 1 celery stalk, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 250g (½ lb) ground beef
- 250g (½ lb) ground pork
- 100ml (⅓ cup) dry red wine
- 400g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- Salt and pepper to taste
- A few basil leaves or a bay leaf
- *Optional:* a splash of milk (to soften the acidity)

#### For the Béchamel Sauce:
- 4 tbsp (60g) unsalted butter
- 4 tbsp (30g) all-purpose flour
- 3 cups (700ml) whole milk, warmed
- Freshly grated nutmeg (just a pinch)
- Salt to taste

#### Other:
- Fresh lasagne sheets (or dried, pre-cooked ones)
- 100g (1 cup) grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
- Butter (for greasing and dotting on top)


---

### 👩‍🍳 Instructions

#### Step 1: Make the Ragù
1. Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot.
2. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Sauté on medium-low until soft (about 8 minutes).
3. Stir in garlic and cook briefly until fragrant.
4. Add beef and pork. Brown the meat, breaking it up with a wooden spoon.
5. Pour in red wine and let it reduce until mostly evaporated.
6. Add crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir in basil or bay leaf.
7. Simmer gently, uncovered, for at least 1½ hours. Add a splash of milk toward the end to balance acidity.

#### Step 2: Prepare the Béchamel
1. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
2. Whisk in flour to form a smooth roux. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly.
3. Gradually whisk in the warm milk. Continue whisking until thick and smooth.
4. Season with salt and a pinch of nutmeg.

#### Step 3: Assemble the Lasagne
1. Preheat oven to 180°C / 350°F.
2. Lightly grease a baking dish with butter.
3. Start layering: a spoon of ragù, a layer of pasta, ragù, béchamel, a sprinkle of Parmigiano. Repeat for 4–5 layers.
4. Finish with béchamel and extra Parmigiano on top. Dot with a few small pieces of butter.

#### Step 4: Bake
- Cover loosely with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
- Uncover and bake another 15–20 minutes until bubbling and golden.
- Let rest for at least 15 minutes before serving.

---

### 🍷 Serving Suggestion
Serve with a simple arugula salad and a glass of **Chianti Classico** or **Barbera**. Lasagne is even better the next day!

---

### 🤌 Chef's Tip
For an extra-authentic touch, use **fresh egg pasta** sheets and simmer your ragù for 3+ hours — just like in Bologna.
