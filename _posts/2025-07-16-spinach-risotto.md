_A creamy, vibrant green risotto made with fresh spinach, arborio rice, and a touch of Parmigiano. Elegant enough for guests, comforting enough for a quiet night in._

---

## 📝 Ingredients

- 1 tbsp olive oil  
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter  
- 1 small onion, finely diced  
- 1 clove garlic, minced  
- 1 cup arborio rice  
- 1/3 cup dry white wine  
- 1.2L (5 cups) vegetable or chicken broth, kept warm  
- 150g (5 oz) fresh spinach leaves  
- 30g (¼ cup) grated Parmigiano-Reggiano  
- Zest of ½ lemon (optional, but recommended)  
- Salt and black pepper to taste

---

## 👩‍🍳 Instructions

### Step 1: Blanch the Spinach
1. Bring a small pot of water to boil. Add the spinach and cook for 30–40 seconds until wilted.
2. Drain and immediately plunge into ice water to retain color.
3. Squeeze out excess water and purée in a blender or food processor until smooth. Set aside.

### Step 2: Cook the Risotto
1. In a large saucepan, heat olive oil and 1 tbsp butter over medium heat.
2. Add onion and cook until translucent (about 4–5 minutes).
3. Stir in garlic, then add the rice. Toast it for 1–2 minutes until slightly translucent at the edges.
4. Pour in white wine and stir until absorbed.
5. Begin adding the warm broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding more.
6. Continue until the rice is tender but slightly al dente, about 18–20 minutes.

### Step 3: Finish the Dish
1. Stir in the spinach purée and remaining butter.
2. Add Parmigiano and lemon zest, stirring to combine.
3. Season to taste with salt and black pepper.

---

## 🍽️ Serving Suggestion

Top with a few shavings of Parmigiano and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Pair with a crisp white wine like **Verdicchio** or **Pinot Grigio**.

---

## 🌿 Chef's Tip

For an even silkier finish, blend a spoon of mascarpone into the risotto just before serving. It adds richness without overpowering the spinach.
